Venus, often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition, is a fascinating and complex planet in our solar system. Here are some key points about Venus:

General Characteristics

- **Size**: Venus has a diameter of about 12,104 kilometers (7,521 miles), making it the second-largest terrestrial planet after Earth. - **Orbit**: It orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 108 million kilometers (67 million miles) and takes about 225 Earth days to complete one orbit.

Surface and Atmosphere

- **Surface**: Venus has a rocky surface with mountains, valleys, and extensive volcanic plains. The surface temperature is extremely high, averaging around 467°C (872°F), making it the hottest planet in the solar system. - **Atmosphere**: Venus has a thick atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide (about 96.5%) and nitrogen (about 3.5%), with clouds of sulfuric acid. This dense atmosphere creates an intense greenhouse effect, trapping heat.

Rotation

- **Day Length**: A day on Venus (one complete rotation on its axis) lasts about 243 Earth days, which is longer than its orbital period. Interestingly, it rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, resulting in the Sun rising in the west and setting in the east.

Exploration

- Venus has been explored by several missions, including NASA's Magellan spacecraft, which mapped its surface, and the Soviet Venera missions, which successfully landed on the planet and returned data.

Unique Features

- **Surface Pressure**: The atmospheric pressure at the surface of Venus is about 92 times that of Earth's, equivalent to being nearly a kilometer underwater. - **Volcanism**: There is evidence of volcanic activity, with many large volcanoes and lava flows, but it’s unclear whether they are currently active. - **Temperature Variability**: Despite its proximity to the Sun, Venus has a relatively uniform temperature across its surface, thanks to its thick atmosphere.
